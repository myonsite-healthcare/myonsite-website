How Decentralized Clinical Trials Are Changing Patient Participation Forever

February 12, 2024 6 min read myOnsite Healthcare Team

Clinical trials have traditionally required participants to visit research sites multiple times — often over months or years. For many patients, especially those who are elderly, immunocompromised, or live far from academic medical centers, this burden is simply too high. The result? High screen failure rates, poor diversity, and costly retention challenges that slow drug development for everyone.

Decentralized clinical trials (DCTs) are changing this by bringing the research to participants, not the other way around.

What Is a Decentralized Clinical Trial?

A decentralized clinical trial uses technology and mobile services to conduct all or part of a study outside of a traditional research site. This includes remote consent via eConsent platforms, wearable devices for continuous data collection, telemedicine visits with investigators, and — critically — mobile specimen collection services like those provided by myOnsite Healthcare.

Rather than requiring a patient with advanced cancer to travel 90 minutes for a routine blood draw, a certified mobile phlebotomist arrives at their home with all necessary study equipment, follows the sponsor protocol exactly, and ships samples to the central lab with validated cold-chain management.

The Impact on Patient Diversity and Enrollment

One of the most significant benefits of DCTs is improved diversity in clinical research. Minority populations, patients in rural areas, and working adults have historically been underrepresented in clinical trials — largely due to travel barriers. By removing those barriers, sponsors see higher enrollment rates, faster trial completion, and data that better reflects the real-world patient population.

Key Statistic: Studies show that decentralized approaches can reduce patient dropout rates by up to 40% and cut overall trial costs by 25-30% compared to traditional site-based models.

How myOnsite Healthcare Supports DCTs

As a certified mobile Site Management Organization (mSMO), myOnsite Healthcare provides end-to-end mobile specimen collection for clinical trials across 7 states. Our GCP-trained staff handle protocol-specific blood draws, urine collections, tissue samples, and biospecimen processing — all documented in electronic chain-of-custody systems.

We work directly with sponsors, CROs, and academic medical centers to integrate seamlessly into existing trial infrastructure, manage kit logistics, and ensure 99.8%+ sample integrity rates on every collection.

The Future Is Patient-Centric

The FDA's guidance on decentralized clinical trials, released in 2023, signals a permanent shift toward patient-centric research models. Sponsors who embrace mobile site services today will have a significant competitive advantage in recruitment, retention, and data quality for years to come.

At myOnsite Healthcare, we believe that every patient deserves access to innovative therapies — and every research team deserves a reliable mobile partner to make that possible.
