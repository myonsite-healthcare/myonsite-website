FAQ

Common questions

A certified phlebotomist visits your home, office, or facility at a scheduled time to collect your blood sample. The sample is transported to a CLIA-certified lab for analysis, and results are shared with your physician within 24–48 hours.
Many major insurance plans cover mobile phlebotomy, including Medicare and Medicaid under qualifying conditions. Contact us with your insurance details and we will verify your benefits before your appointment at no charge.
For most tests, fast for 8–12 hours prior. Stay well-hydrated with water, avoid strenuous exercise, and wear loose-fitting clothing with easy arm access. Your phlebotomist will confirm specific preparation when you book.
Most standard lab results are delivered to your physician within 24–48 hours of collection. Specialized tests may take 3–5 business days. Your doctor will then review and share results with you per their normal process.
We currently serve California, Connecticut, Florida, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, and New York. We're actively expanding — call us to check availability in your area even if your state isn't listed.
