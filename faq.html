Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about our mobile lab and clinical trial services.

General
What is myOnsite Healthcare?
myOnsite Healthcare is a mobile phlebotomy and clinical laboratory services company that sends certified healthcare professionals directly to your home, office, assisted living facility, or wherever you are located. We eliminate the need to travel to a lab or hospital for blood draws and other specimen collections.
What states do you serve?
We currently serve patients in California, Nevada, Arizona, Texas, Florida, New York, and Washington. We are continuously expanding — contact us to check availability in your specific city or zip code.
How do I schedule an appointment?
You can schedule an appointment by calling us at 1 (888) 566-9990, emailing us at info@myonsitehealthcare.com, or filling out the contact form on our website. We offer same-day and next-day appointments in most service areas, Monday through Saturday.
What are your hours of operation?
Our mobile phlebotomists are available Monday–Friday from 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM and Saturday from 8:00 AM to 4:00 PM (local time). Our office support team is available during these same hours to assist with scheduling, billing questions, and more.
Services
What types of lab tests can you collect?
We collect specimens for virtually all standard laboratory tests ordered by your physician, including complete blood count (CBC), comprehensive metabolic panels (CMP), lipid panels, HbA1c, thyroid panels, coagulation studies (INR/PT/PTT), hormone panels, allergy testing, infectious disease panels, and many more. Our phlebotomists follow strict chain-of-custody protocols to ensure accurate results.
Do you handle anti-coagulation monitoring?
Yes! We have a dedicated anti-coagulation management program. Patients on warfarin, heparin, or other blood thinners can have their INR/PT levels monitored from home. Results are sent directly to the ordering physician for dosage adjustments, avoiding unnecessary clinic visits.
Can you support clinical trials?
Absolutely. We are a preferred mobile site management organization (mSMO) for clinical research. We handle Phase I–IV trial specimen collection, kit preparation, temperature-controlled specimen handling, chain-of-custody documentation, and courier coordination to central labs — all following strict ICH-GCP and sponsor protocols.
Do you offer corporate wellness and event services?
Yes. We partner with corporations, senior living communities, health fairs, and community organizations to provide on-site health screenings, flu shot programs, COVID testing, wellness panel blood draws, and other preventive health services. Contact us to get a custom quote for your event.
Insurance & Billing
Do you accept insurance?
Yes, we accept most major insurance plans including Medicare, Medi-Cal, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, Humana, and many others. Our billing team will verify your benefits before your appointment. Please have your insurance card and a copy of your lab order ready.
Is there a fee for the home visit?
A mobile convenience fee may apply depending on your insurance plan and location. Many insurance plans cover the mobile draw fee entirely. We will transparently communicate any out-of-pocket costs before scheduling your appointment. We also offer self-pay options with competitive pricing.
How will I receive my lab results?
Lab results are sent directly to your ordering physician, typically within 24–48 hours depending on the test. Your doctor will then contact you to discuss results. For some routine tests, we can also provide a copy of results directly to you upon request, subject to your physician's authorization.
Safety & Qualifications
Are your phlebotomists certified?
All of our mobile phlebotomists are certified by nationally recognized organizations (NPA, ASCP, or AMT), have passed comprehensive background checks, and undergo regular skills validation. They follow OSHA bloodborne pathogen standards and maintain up-to-date healthcare certifications including CPR/BLS.
How do you ensure specimen quality and integrity?
We follow strict pre-analytical protocols including proper patient identification with two identifiers, correct tube selection and order of draw, temperature-controlled transport with validated cold-chain management, and time-sensitive sample handling. All specimens are tracked from collection to delivery at the reference laboratory.
Is it safe to have a blood draw at home?
Yes, absolutely. Home blood draws by certified phlebotomists are extremely safe. Our team brings all necessary supplies including proper sharps disposal containers, PPE, sterile needles, and collection tubes. After collection, all biohazardous waste is properly contained and disposed of according to state and federal regulations.
